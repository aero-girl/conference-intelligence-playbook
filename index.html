Conference Intelligence Engine · Gavi Playbook

Don’t collect transcripts. Build a live advantage.

Otter, notes and photos are only raw material. The win is turning every session into team intelligence, client insight, learning loops, follow-ups and public CAIO thought leadership.

Is the skill enough?

No — not on its own. The skill is the operating brain, but the best setup needs four extra pieces:

  • a live capture routine so nothing rots in Otter,
  • a repeatable output stack for teams and public content,
  • a day-end synthesis rhythm,
  • and a public/private publishing lane.

The principle

Every talk should become at least one useful artefact: a team brief, a client angle, a learning card, a follow-up, a post, a demo idea, or a claim to verify.

If a transcript produces only a summary, we left value on the floor.

Live session routine

  1. Start Otter and label the session.
  2. Take photos of only the useful slides.
  3. Write 3 messy notes: 🔥, client, post.
  4. After the talk, record a 60-second reaction.
  5. Upload transcript + notes + photos here.
  6. I turn it into the output stack.

60-second voice note script

“The session was about ____. The useful bit was ____. I think this matters because ____. It connects to my work on ____. I disagree with ____. This could become a post about ____.”

That personal reaction is the difference between bland transcript summary and real thought leadership.

X / social signal layer

Conference transcripts tell us what was said in the room. Social signal tells us what the market is arguing about outside it. Use X posts, comments, public LinkedIn/news/blogs and the xAI/Grok search skill to find the strongest opinions, objections and customer-ready angles.

FSI spin

Translate the conference into trust, risk, governance, audit evidence, model assurance, data lineage, fraud, customer service, and regulated advice.

Example line: “For banks, the question is moving from ‘can we build AI?’ to ‘can we prove how it behaved under pressure?’”

Retail / CPG spin

Translate it into AI shelf, product content, recommendation engines, customer agents, supply chain, personalisation, and brand visibility inside AI answers.

Example line: “Retailers won’t just compete for shelf space — they’ll compete for recommendation space inside AI assistants.”
Safety rule: use public real-world examples where possible. Don’t replay private/off-record material, don’t show slide photos unless permitted, and label unverified speaker claims as claims.

Posting rules

  • Don’t publish private / Chatham House content.
  • Don’t quote speakers unless clearly allowed.
  • Mark vendor stats as claims until verified.
  • Keep photos internal unless sharing is permitted.
  • Use Gavi’s voice: sharp, warm, concrete, not corporate soup.
